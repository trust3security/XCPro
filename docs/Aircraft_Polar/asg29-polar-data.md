## Water Ballast System

### Tank Configuration
| Tank Location | Capacity (liters) |
|---------------|------------------|
| Wing tanks | 170 (total) |
| Fuselage tank (optional) | 30 |
| **Total Capacity** | **200** |

### Ballast Recommendations
- Fill wing tanks evenly to prevent asymmetry
- Dump time: Approximately 3-4 minutes for full load
- Consider partial ballast (25-50%) for winch launching
- Full ballast suitable for aerotow only

### Effect on Performance
| Ballast % | Wing Loading (kg/m^2) | Best L/D Speed (km/h) |

## Engine Options

### ASG 29 E - Sustainer
- **Engine:** Solo 2350
- **Power:** 18 hp (13.4 kW)
- **Type:** 2-stroke, 2-cylinder
- **Climb Rate:** 1.2 m/s (236 ft/min) unballasted
- **Climb Rate with ballast:** Significantly reduced, ~0.5 m/s or less
- **Not certified for self-launch**

### ASG 29 Es - Electric Sustainer
- **Type:** Electric motor with folding propeller
- **Battery:** Lithium batteries
- **Endurance:** Limited by battery capacity
- **Climb Rate:** Similar to Solo 2350

## Important Notes

### Handling Characteristics
- Excellent handling across entire speed range
- More difficult to handle with full ballast during ground run
- Cross-wind particularly problematic with ballast
- Remains flyable at all flap and ballast configurations

### Competition Performance
- Multiple World Championship wins
- 2014 World Champion (Karol Staryszak)
- 2006 World Championship 1st & 2nd place
- Record flight: 1582 km at 144 km/h average

### Safety Considerations
1. **Water Ballast:** Affects handling significantly, especially on ground
2. **Sustainer Engine:** Not powerful enough for takeoff, emergency use only
3. **High Performance:** Requires experienced pilot for optimal use
4. **Speed-to-Fly:** Changes significantly with ballast load
