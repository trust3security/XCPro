## LX Variometer Setup

### Method 1: Three-Point Polar Entry
Enter three speed/sink pairs based on your typical flying weight:

#### Example for 18m at 500kg:
```
V1: 85 km/h   W1: 0.63 m/s
V2: 110 km/h  W2: 0.72 m/s
V3: 170 km/h  W3: 1.60 m/s
```

#### Example for 21m at 600kg:
```
V1: 80 km/h   W1: 0.62 m/s
V2: 110 km/h  W2: 0.68 m/s
V3: 170 km/h  W3: 1.35 m/s
```

### Method 2: Polar Coefficients
The polar equation: **w = a + bxV + cxV^2**

Typical coefficients (adjust based on actual weight):
- Calculate from polar points using regression analysis
- Coefficients change with wing loading

### Required LX Settings
1. **Glider Empty Weight:** 330 kg
2. **Pilot Weight:** Your actual weight with equipment
3. **Water Ballast:** Current amount in liters
4. **Wing Area:** 11.19 m^2 (18m) or 12.25 m^2 (21m)
5. **Bugs Factor:** 0-20% degradation

### MacCready Settings
- Weak conditions: 0-1.0 m/s
- Moderate conditions: 1.0-2.5 m/s
- Strong conditions: 2.5-4.0 m/s
- Booming conditions: 4.0+ m/s

## Special Features Affecting Performance

1. **Double-hole blowing/suction system** on lower wing surface
2. **Turbulator tape** on tailplane and rudder (essential for control)
3. **Mylar/Teflon control gap seals** (must be maintained)
4. **Polyhedral wing shape** allows high bank angles on landing
5. **Third-generation winglets** (21m configuration)

## Altitude Corrections

### VNE Reduction with Altitude
- Sea level to 3000m: Full VNE
- Above 3000m: Progressive reduction required
- Maximum operating altitude: 9000m (30,000 ft)

## Important Warnings

1. **Water Ballast Temperature:** Dump all water if OAT < 0degC
2. **Asymmetric Water:** Land immediately if asymmetry detected
3. **21m Tips:** Must be filled if main tanks have any water
4. **Spin Limitations:** No intentional spins in 21m or with water
5. **Control Freezing:** Move controls regularly in cold conditions
